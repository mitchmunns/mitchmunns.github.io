What do the symbols mean?

Every bit of notation used on this page, plain and simple.

x̄"x-bar" — a mean. The average of a set of scores: add them up, divide by how many there are.
x̄grandGrand mean. The average of every score from every active group, pooled together into one big list.
sStandard deviation. Roughly, how far a typical score sits from its own group's mean — the "spread" of one group.
nGroup size. The number of scores in one particular group.
NTotal sample size. The number of scores across every active group combined.
kNumber of groups. How many distributions are currently active (1–4 here).
Σ"Sigma" — sum of. Add up whatever comes after it, once for every score.
SSSum of Squares. Take each score's deviation from a mean, square it (so negatives don't cancel positives), then add all those squares up. It's the raw amount of "spread-out-ness."
SSwWithin-group SS. Add up (score − its own group's mean)² for every score, in every group. This is variability that has nothing to do with group membership — just noise.
SSbBetween-group SS. For each group, take n × (that group's mean − the grand mean)², then add those up across groups. This is variability that comes from the groups being different.
SStTotal SS. Add up (score − grand mean)² for every score, ignoring which group it's in. It always equals SSw + SSb — the total spread splits perfectly into "because of group" and "just noise."
dfDegrees of freedom. How many values were actually free to vary. df-between = k − 1, df-within = N − k, df-total = N − 1.
MSMean Square. An SS turned into an average — a proper variance estimate — by dividing by its df: MS = SS ÷ df.
FF-ratio. MS-between ÷ MS-within. If the groups' means are truly different, the between-group variance tends to dwarf the within-group noise, so F climbs well above 1.
